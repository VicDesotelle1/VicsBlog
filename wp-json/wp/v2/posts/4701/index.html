Have you ever noticed how video ratings get very close but rarely hit \u201c5\u201d as an average? Well, these 3 videos hit that big \u20185\u2019 rating, with the first two presently around a million views, and the third \u2018hugs\u2019 video having over 57 million. Why do these videos have such huge ratings and viewings? I propose an answer:<\/span><\/span><\/p>\n

In these views videos, our humanity is spoken to<\/span>. Not our processes, or our procedures, or someone\u2019s organizational goals or strategic plans. But instead, these videos move us at the deepest core of our human nature.<\/p>\n

1-The Gift of an Ordinary Day<\/a>
\n<\/strong>
\n2-Helsinki Complaints Choir<\/a>
\n<\/strong>
\n3-Free Hugs Campaign<\/a><\/strong><\/p>\n

I bring this forward because I believe that our discussions and planning about collaboration, and innovation, and the future, and ‘changing normal’, are not spiced with enough of a very essential ingredient, which is this: We are each passionate, passionate people simply awaiting to be amazed by the next moment of ‘awe’ that comes with a sunrise. Each of us<\/span> – no matter what our situation, crisis, gain, loss, have, or have-not – is deeply in love with life.<\/span> I guess what I\u2019m asking here is that you watch these 3 videos and allow yourself to \u2018fall in love\u2019. Allow yourself to feel the welling of your own emotions as they arise within you. And then, THEN let us ask as a collective: What is collaboration and innovation? What is community? And, why in the world are we wanting to ‘change normal’?<\/p>\n


\n<\/strong> <\/span><\/span><\/p><\/blockquote>\n

In Spirit,
\nVic Desotelle
